The process

Three steps. Sixty seconds. One clear answer.

  1. 01

    Scan

    Enter your email. BreachAlert checks it against known breach databases and audits any passwords you choose to test — locally, never stored in plain text.

  2. 02

    Score

    We turn the results into a single score out of 100, weighted by real risk — an old breach on an unused account counts less than an exposed password you still use today.

    Fix

    Get a short, ranked list of what to change first. Come back anytime to re-scan and watch your score improve.

Why it matters

Most breaches don't start with hacking. They start with reuse.

A single reused password on one forgotten account is often the only thing standing between a stranger and your inbox, your bank, and everything connected to them. BreachAlert exists because most people don't find out they're exposed until it's too late — and because knowing your risk shouldn't require a computer science degree or a monthly subscription.

Good questions

Frequently asked questions

Do you ever see or store my password?

No. Password strength is checked entirely in your browser before anything is sent anywhere. The password itself never leaves your device — only the resulting strength score does.

How do you check for breaches without storing my email?

Your email is checked against breach records using a one-way hash, not the plain address. We don't keep a log of what you searched once the scan finishes, unless you choose to save your score.

Is BreachAlert really free?

Yes. Scanning, scoring, and one-time breach checks are free with no account required. Saving your history and getting ongoing alerts needs a free account, but there's no paid tier hidden behind a paywall.

What do I do if I find a breach?

Your results page ranks fixes by urgency — usually: change the exposed password, turn on two-factor authentication, and stop reusing that password anywhere else. We link you straight to the account settings page where you can do it.
